Introduction

This project explores how YouTube channels perform by analysing a dataset containing uploads, views, subscribers, and channel attributes. The aim was to understand what drives engagement and how viewer behaviour shifts across categories. Using Power BI, the raw data was shaped into a dashboard that tells a clear and practical story about performance on the platform.

Objective

The purpose of the analysis was to identify meaningful patterns and trends within YouTube engagement data. The focus was on understanding how channels grow, which categories attract the most attention, and what separates high-performing content from the rest. The insights guide creators and marketing teams toward more informed content decisions.

Methodology

The dataset retrieved through YouTube's API went through a full ETL process before visualisation. The data was cleaned to handle missing values, standardised for consistency, and modelled to ensure accurate calculations.

After preparation, several visual elements were built to highlight different aspects of performance. Trend lines, rank comparisons, category-based visuals, and custom metrics were combined into an interactive dashboard. A calculated measure for "Views per Subscriber" added an extra layer of clarity when comparing engagement efficiency across channels.

Results

The dashboard revealed noticeable differences in how channels attract and retain viewers. High-upload creators stood out through clear spikes in engagement. Patterns across categories became easier to interpret, showing where subscriber interest is strongest.

The newly created engagement metric helped identify channels that not only gather subscribers but also convert them into active viewers.

Overall, the final dashboard provided a concise and actionable view of YouTube performance, making it useful for anyone looking to sharpen their content strategy.
